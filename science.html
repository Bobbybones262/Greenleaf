How Cannabis Affects the Body and Brain

Cannabis contains hundreds of chemical compounds called cannabinoids. The two most well-known are:

  • THC (Tetrahydrocannabinol): The main psychoactive component that produces the "high". It binds to cannabinoid receptors in the brain.
  • CBD (Cannabidiol): Non-psychoactive, known for potential therapeutic benefits like reducing anxiety and inflammation.

The Endocannabinoid System

The human body has an endocannabinoid system (ECS) which regulates mood, appetite, pain sensation, memory, and more. Cannabis cannabinoids interact with ECS receptors, influencing these functions.

Effects on the Brain

  • Euphoria, relaxation, altered perception (mostly THC)
  • Reduced anxiety and seizures (CBD)
  • Impaired short-term memory and coordination (THC)

Medical Research

Cannabis is being studied for treatment of chronic pain, epilepsy, PTSD, multiple sclerosis, and cancer-related symptoms. More research is ongoing to fully understand benefits and risks.
